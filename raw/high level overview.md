Ground work  
Survival \- only included as part of escape format.  Does not have it’s own week.

* Dealing with pressure, establishing frames

Escapes and reversals

* Will be defined by Early, middle, and late stage options

Guard \- closed, dela riva, single leg x, x, spider, butterfly, lapel, collar sleeve

* Offensive cycle \- sweeps and submissions  
* Defensive cycle \- retention and recoveries

Passing

* Style: pressure, movement, submission based  
* Staging areas, headquarters, split squat, half guard, etc

Pins, submissions and transitions \- categorize per week by secure/control, attack/submit, advance/transition

* Mount  
* Side control, all variations including kesa gatame and reverse kesa gatame  
* North south  
* Turtle  
* Rear mount

Submissions 1 option per week that aligns with weekly ground technical portion.

* Offensive cycle \- attacking and chaining submissions   
* Defensive cycle \- defending and escaping submission attempts

Last two weeks

* Youth will focus on self defence, standing and ground  
* Adults will focus on leg locks, offensive and defensive cycles

Design a curriculum, starting with the youth curriculum, that takes into account all the information above along with industry best practices, and in alignment with the kin bjj compendium.  Also in congruence with the details below.

* Each week should have a section for takedowns, ground work, and submissions.  Keep offensive and defensive cycles together as much as possible.    
* Each section should also have a section for level 1 and 2, where the latter offers more complex options, transitions or chains.  
* Each section should contain some details about each move that would be useful for a coach to teach  
* Each week should have a theme where all moves flow together in the same way they would in a match.  As example, a hip throw or harai goshi landing in kesa gatame leading to an americana.  
* Alternate every 2 weeks from offensive to defensive cycles  
* Total length of curriculum should be 24 weeks
