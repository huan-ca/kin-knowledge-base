# Week 5 (Offensive Cycle)
- Theme: Snap Down to Front Headlock Fundamentals
- Teaching Goal: Build a connected snap down into front headlock control into front headlock to spin-behind top control sequence.
- Class Length: 60 minutes

## Opening Script
Introduce the theme: Snap Down to Front Headlock Fundamentals. Tell the class the goal is to connect the feet-to-floor entry with stable control on the ground.

## Warm-Up Options
- **Movement Based**:
  - Stance, level change, and movement-prep warm-up.
- **Drill Based**:
  - Partner entry reps with low resistance and quick resets.

## Lesson: Snap Down to Front Headlock Fundamentals
- **Takedown**
  - Coaching Tip: Use snaps to create posture reactions, not just speed.
  - Level 1: Snap down into front headlock control.
  - Level 2: Add timing, angle change, or chain-attack follow-up without increasing chaos.
  - Outcome: Finish to a clean top position that leads into front headlock to spin-behind top control
- **Ground: Front headlock to spin-behind top control**
  - Secure: Front headlock to spin-behind top control.
  - Level 2: Add a simple transition or pressure-retention task before the finish.
- **Submission: Guillotine threat or positional conversion**
  - Cue: Guillotine threat or positional conversion.
- **Situational Options**
  - Start from the target control point and rotate on clear win conditions.
  - Run short rounds with a fast reset after the first clean win condition or escape.

## Coach Notes
- Adult framing: Use adult pacing and decision-making language.
- Use snaps to create posture reactions, not just speed.
- Keep the room moving with short explanation windows and clear reset points.

## Closing Script
- Recap the theme: Snap Down to Front Headlock Fundamentals.
- Reinforce one safety cue and one successful habit from the room.
