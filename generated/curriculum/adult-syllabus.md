# Adult Program Syllabus

This syllabus maps the adult weekly sequence to its cycle, theme, description, and main goal.

| Week | Cycle | Theme | Description | Main Goal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 01 | defensive | Early Escape Frames From Mount And Side Control | Early-stage escape structure from mount and side control. | Reinforce calm survival habits and first-stage escapes from the most common major pins. |
| 02 | defensive | Late Escape Chains And Safe Guard Recovery | Middle and late-stage escape chains from mount, knee on belly, and heavy side control. | Prepare adults to recover from failed first escapes and restore guard under realistic top pressure. |
| 03 | offensive | Closed Guard Control And Simple Sweeps | Closed guard posture breaking and hip-bump style sweep threats. | Reestablish closed guard as a serious offensive hub by linking posture breaking and basic sweep threats. |
| 04 | offensive | Closed Guard Submission Chains | Closed guard armbar and triangle pathway with controlled finishing details. | Create one reliable attacking sequence from closed guard with clean submission mechanics. |
| 05 | defensive | Open Guard Retention And Knee-Elbow Recovery | Open guard retention, hip turns, and knee-elbow recovery against passing pressure. | Sharpen retention and recovery mechanics against pressure and movement passing. |
| 06 | defensive | Seated And De La Riva Recovery Reactions | Recovering seated guard and De La Riva hooks after the passer clears the first line. | Use seated and De La Riva reactions to restore usable guard structure after grips break. |
| 07 | offensive | Headquarters Passing And Knee-Cut Entry | Headquarters posture, leg stapling, and knee-cut passing. | Connect headquarters structure to a controlled passing lane that finishes with chest pressure. |
| 08 | offensive | Toreando And Leg-Drag Passing Chains | Passing around the legs with toreando and leg-drag concepts. | Grow passing options around the legs while still demanding strong finishing control. |
| 09 | defensive | Side Control Escape Layers And Kesa Survival | Escapes from side control, kesa gatame, and transition pressure. | Handle side control pressure and scarf-hold variants without overexposing the back or arms. |
| 10 | defensive | Mount And Knee-On-Belly Escape Chains | Defensive reactions from mount and knee on belly into half guard or full guard recovery. | Link major-pin escapes into repeatable chains that hold up under heavier pace. |
| 11 | offensive | Side Control Control To Americana | Side control pressure, far-side isolation, and americana mechanics. | Use top control to build one reliable attack route instead of a loose collection of submissions. |
| 12 | offensive | Mount Progression To Armbar And Collar Attack | Mount control, high mount progression, and armbar or collar-based attack entries. | Connect mount climbing, isolation, and attack selection under realistic defensive reactions. |
| 13 | defensive | Turtle Posture, Sit-Outs, And Front-Headlock Defense | Turtle structure, sit-outs, and defensive reactions to front-headlock pressure. | Use turtle defensively without letting it become a dead-end shell. |
| 14 | defensive | Back Survival, Hand Fighting, And Rear-Escape Chains | Back defense, two-on-one hand fighting, and escaping to safe top or guard positions. | Organize adult back defense around hand fighting, shoulder line, and hook removal. |
| 15 | offensive | Rear Mount Control To Strangle Finish | Rear mount control, hook management, and clean rear-strangle mechanics. | Turn rear control into a high-percentage finishing system built on patient control and clean mechanics. |
| 16 | offensive | North-South Control And Transition Attacks | North-south control, spin transitions, and submission entry timing. | Use north-south as both a control point and a route to chained attacks. |
| 17 | defensive | Grip Fighting, Stance, And Sprawl Reactions | Grip fighting, stance discipline, level-change awareness, and sprawls. | Use standing defense to build confidence against pressure and takedown attempts. |
| 18 | defensive | Takedown Counters To Safe Top Entries | Countering common takedown attempts and landing in stable top control. | Move from stopping shots to converting defensive wins into usable top position. |
| 19 | offensive | Inside Reaps, Ouchi, And Osoto To Top Control | Inside trips and outer reaps that land directly into side control or scarf-hold pressure. | Tie basic takedown families to immediate top stabilization and pin awareness. |
| 20 | offensive | Double Leg And Body-Lock Entries To Passing Finish | Double leg or body-lock entry leading to stable top position and first pass. | Connect wrestling-style entries to immediate top control and passing follow-through. |
| 21 | defensive | Armbar And Triangle Defense | Defensive posture and escape routes against armbar and triangle attempts. | Use posture and angle awareness to survive and unwind common upper-body submission threats. |
| 22 | defensive | Choke Defense, Tap Awareness, And Reset Reactions | Defending rear strangles, collar-based threats, and recovering composure after the first defense. | Finish the defensive cycle with clear neck-safety rules, hand-fighting reactions, and calm reset behavior. |
| 23 | offensive | Lower-Body Offense: Entries, Ashi Position, And Controlled Finishes | Safe leg-entanglement entries, ashi positioning, and controlled straight-footlock style finishing mechanics. | Introduce lower-body submission offense through safe entries, control, and pressure management. |
| 24 | defensive | Lower-Body Defense: Hand Fighting, Escapes, And Counter-Attacks | Lower-body submission defense through hand fighting, knee-line clearing, and safe positional recovery. | Finish the adult cycle with practical leg-lock defense, escape priorities, and safe countering concepts. |
