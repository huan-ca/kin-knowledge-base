# Side Control System

## Definition
Side control is presented as a stable control family with multiple variants built around chest pressure, upper-body isolation, and transition options.

## Key Details
- Variants include traditional cross-face and underhook, both-arms-far-side, overhook and near-side-hip, cross-face and near-side-hip, kesa gatame, and reverse kesa gatame.
- The source maps each variant through secure, advance, and attack lenses.
- Side control is also a hub for transitions to mount, north-south, back takes, and shoulder attacks.

## Gaps
- The source offers rich content, but not yet a gym-approved age progression for each side-control variant.
