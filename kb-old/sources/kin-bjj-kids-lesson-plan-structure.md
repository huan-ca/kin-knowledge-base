## Summary
This source is an operations-facing teaching guide for kids classes. It expands class structure, life-skills conversations, games, drilling, competition-session design, physical preparation, mat safety, community building, and closing procedures.

## Extracted Claims
- Tots classes target ages 4 to 7 and youth classes target ages 8 to 13, with students assessed individually. (`chunk-001`)
- Recommended class durations are 30 minutes for tots and 60 minutes for youth. (`chunk-002`)
- The youth class template includes welcome, warm up, takedown teaching and drilling, ground teaching and drilling, sparring, and closing recognition. (`chunk-003`)
- Coaches should use positive reinforcement, clear behavior boundaries, and assistant coaches to preserve class flow. (`chunk-004`)
- Life skills chat can cover challenges, adversity, mindset, helping others, excellence, integrity, and respect. (`chunk-005`)
- Games should be BJJ-specific where possible and can be used to reward attention and effort. (`chunk-006`)
- Technical lesson delivery should be short, structured, and repeated with progressive resistance. (`chunk-007`)
- Drilling is framed as the bridge between demonstration and sparring and includes solo, call-out, pocket, objective, and reset drills. (`chunk-008`)
- Uke and tori roles should both be active, with defined resistance expectations. (`chunk-009`)
- Competition classes should use invite-only, higher-intensity sessions with rules review, targeted drilling, and open rounds. (`chunk-010`)
- General physical preparedness includes stability, mobility, flexibility, strength, speed or agility, and coordination drills. (`chunk-011`)
- Mat safety requires supervision, first-aid readiness, partner safety norms, submission safety, and progressive exposure for beginners. (`chunk-012`)
- Community, announcements, and recognition are part of retention and culture building, not just class administration. (`chunk-013`)

## Open Questions
