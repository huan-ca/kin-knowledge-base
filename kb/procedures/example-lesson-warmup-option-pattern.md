# Example Lesson Warm-Up Option Pattern

## Definition
The new scripted lesson-plan examples use warm-ups that stay tightly related to the lesson theme while varying by audience and class density.

## Detailed Notes
- The adult and youth examples group warm-up ideas into movement-based, drill-based, and CLA-based options instead of a single required script.
- The adult and youth movement options include duck walks, breakfalls, and bear crawls with backsteps.
- The adult and youth drill options include double-leg pickups and penetration-step work that directly support the takedown theme.
- The tots example uses a simpler movement-led warm-up with one named playful example rather than a menu of option categories.
- Across the examples, the warm-up is positioned as a direct setup for the lesson rather than a separate conditioning block.

## Gaps
- The sources do not define decision rules for when a coach should pick movement-based, drill-based, or CLA-based warm-up options.
- The sources do not establish whether CLA-based warm-up content is standard house language or just an illustrative placeholder in these examples.
