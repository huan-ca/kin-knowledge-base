# Technical Lesson Delivery Model

## Definition
New technique instruction should follow a short, repeatable sequence that prioritizes clarity and controlled repetition over long explanations.

## Detailed Notes
- The core principle is that explanations should stay short enough to preserve attention and movement quality, especially in youth settings.
- The model starts with a real-time demonstration so students can see the shape of the technique before details are broken apart.
- The breakdown phase should emphasize major mechanics first. The source explicitly warns against drowning students in tiny adjustments too early.
- Students should attempt the movement quickly instead of waiting through long lectures. That first attempt also exposes the most common mistakes.
- Coaches then call the room back, correct those common mistakes, and send the students out again with a better mental picture.
- The lesson does not stop at static repetition. It progresses into minimal resistance and then more live resistance so the technique starts to feel real.

## Operational Implications
- This structure supports short teaching blocks inside larger class flows, which is exactly how the youth class template is organized.
- It also pairs naturally with the drilling methodology page because lesson delivery and drill progression are two halves of the same process.

## Gaps
- The source does not define separate delivery variants for every age or belt cohort.
