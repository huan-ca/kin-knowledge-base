## Summary
This source is a curriculum design brief. It defines the intended shape of the multi-week curriculum, especially how takedowns, groundwork, submissions, offensive and defensive cycles, and level 1 versus level 2 progressions should fit together.

## Design Signals
- Weekly lessons should connect takedowns, ground work, and submissions into one match-like theme.
- Every area should support a level 1 baseline and a level 2 follow-up chain, transition, or more advanced option.
- Offensive and defensive cycles should stay grouped and alternate every two weeks.
- Adult and youth should share a broad structure while diverging near the end around leg-lock versus self-defence emphasis.

## Extracted Claims
- All curriculum should follow the Kin BJJ compendium. (`chunk-001`)
- Takedowns should have level 1 basic options and level 2 advanced or chained options that land into compatible ground work. (`chunk-002`)
- Ground work should cover survival, staged escapes and reversals, guard families, passing styles, and weekly pin or transition themes. (`chunk-003`)
- Submissions should align with the weekly ground technical portion and have offensive and defensive cycles. (`chunk-004`)
- The final two weeks split youth self defence focus from adult leg-lock focus. (`chunk-005`)
- Each week should include takedowns, ground work, and submissions, preserve level 1 and level 2 tracks, and use a match-like technical theme. (`chunk-006`)
- The curriculum should alternate offensive and defensive cycles every two weeks and run for 24 weeks. (`chunk-007`)
