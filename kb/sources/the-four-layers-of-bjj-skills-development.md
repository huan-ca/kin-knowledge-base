# The Four Layers of BJJ Skills Development

## Summary
This source is a coaching and learning-philosophy article describing BJJ development through four categories: techniques, muscle memory, principles, and intuition. It argues that complete development requires all four layers working together rather than isolated technique collection.

## Pedagogical Use
- The article is strongest as a conceptual framework for how coaches should think about student development.
- It supports lesson design that combines demonstration, repetition, conceptual explanation, and live problem solving rather than relying on one teaching mode.
- It also supports evaluating progress in terms broader than memorized techniques.

## Extracted Claims
- The source argues that BJJ development is better understood through four categories: techniques, muscle memory, principles, and intuition. (`chunk-001`)
- Techniques are presented as recipe-like step sequences that give beginners a concrete starting point but do not guarantee live application on their own. (`chunk-002`)
- Muscle memory is developed through repetition, drilling, positional training, and live rolling until movement, timing, pressure, and feel become more automatic. (`chunk-003`)
- Principles explain why techniques work and help students troubleshoot, adapt, and connect apparently separate parts of Jiu-Jitsu. (`chunk-004`)
- Intuition is described as real-time pattern recognition and decision-making that develops slowly through accumulated training experience. (`chunk-005`)
- The best learning outcome comes from developing all four layers together rather than relying on only one. (`chunk-006`)

## Open Questions
- The source is a philosophy article rather than a formal curriculum policy, so downstream use should treat it as a conceptual framework unless later approved as house doctrine.
