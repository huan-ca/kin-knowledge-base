# Youth Submission Safety Framework

## Definition
The submission curriculum is filtered by youth safety and IBJJF legality rather than assuming all common BJJ finishes belong in every kids program.

## Key Details
- The source distinguishes joint locks from chokes.
- Safe or common youth techniques include straight armbar, kimura, triangle with limits, cross collar choke, rear naked choke, and americana depending on position and age.
- Leg locks, wrist locks, neck cranks, and some guillotines or ezekiels are restricted or prohibited for younger athletes.
- Omoplata may be used as a transition rather than a submission in younger divisions.

## Gaps
- The source summarizes youth legal limits, but a gym-facing matrix by age and belt would still improve coach usability.
