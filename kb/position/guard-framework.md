# Guard Framework

## Definition
Guard is treated as both a defensive shield and an offensive platform organized by engagement, secure or control, retention or recovery, sweeps, and submissions.

## Key Details
- The source covers closed guard, open guard, de la riva or reverse de la riva, worm guard, butterfly or seated guard, and half guard.
- Retention and recovery are treated as distinct defensive cycles.
- Sweeps are framed as post removal and weak-corner creation.
- Submissions should come after posture control and structural compromise.

## Gaps
- The source is rich, but a final curriculum still needs to decide which guard families to emphasize first for each age or level.
