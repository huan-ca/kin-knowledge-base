# Escape Framework

## Definition
Escapes are treated as the most important confidence-building layer for kids and are organized around survival, structure, recovery, and reversal.

## Detailed Notes
- The compendium treats escapes as one of the most important confidence-building layers in a young student’s game. If a student cannot survive and recover, the rest of Jiu-Jitsu becomes harder to apply under pressure.
- Major positions such as mount, side control, rear mount, knee on belly, north-south, turtle, and half guard each have their own escape logic, but the shared themes are consistent.
- The common sequence is survival first, then structure or frames, then space creation, then movement into that space for a re-guard, stand-up, or reversal.
- This makes escapes more than “panic reactions.” They are taught as staged responses with early, middle, and late possibilities.

## Operational Implications
- Coaches should be careful not to teach escapes as one magic move. Students need to understand where they are in the exchange and what their first safe job is.
- This framework also aligns cleanly with the curriculum brief’s distinction between survival details and staged escape or reversal options.

## Gaps
- The source contains many escape details, but not a finalized youth progression for which escapes are taught first.
