# Submission Framework

## Definition
Submissions are positioned as the finishing layer of BJJ that must sit on top of posture control, positional dominance, and safety rules.

## Detailed Notes
- The source makes a structural point: submissions are not supposed to float on their own. They sit on top of posture control, positional dominance, and the ability to keep an opponent compromised.
- It distinguishes joint locks from chokes or strangles, which matters for both teaching language and safety emphasis.
- Tap mechanics are part of the framework, not an afterthought. Students are expected to understand how and when to tap, and coaches are expected to normalize controlled application.
- Position-specific examples are given from guard, mount, side control, and rear mount, which reinforces the idea that the route into the submission matters as much as the finish itself.

## Constraints or Safety Notes
- The source is youth-sensitive. Not every legal submission or finishing variation should be treated as equally appropriate for every age group or experience level.
- Submission teaching should be scaled with control expectations, especially for beginners and kids.

## Gaps
- The source offers examples and rules, but still needs final curriculum sequencing and age-band prioritization.
